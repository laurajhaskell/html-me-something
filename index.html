Colombia

November 2017 - My best friend growing up has been teaching English as a secondl language in Periera, Colombia for over a year. I figured the best time to go was when I had a free, spanish speaking travel guide, so I found a cheap flight on Thanksgiving day and stuffed as much of my life as I could in my backpack.

Colombia: Salento

A quick one hour bus ride from Periera, you will find the unique town of Salento. The landsacpe was not like anything I have seen with rolling hills topped with 200ft high tall palm trees and sprinkled with free-roaming horses and cows.

The best way to travel into the valley is by open jeep. No seatbelts. No waviers signed. And the drivers encourage riders to stand on the metal grate attached to the back for the best view and full effect of the rolling hills. May not have been the smartest decision in my life, but I would do it again in a hearbeat.

Colombia: Medellin

To dive deep into the rich culture, we travelled to Colombia's second largest city Medellin. Here the food is amazing and art is everywhere. Local artists were comissioned to cover entire neighborhoods with murals creating widly colorful buildings.
